Knowing Where You Are

A GPS can help locate whereever you are in the world.It can locate you not only on the map but in three dimensions.Photos can be stamped with information about where they were shot as well as when, where and with what camera.

Black Boxes Are Not Just For Airplanes Anymore

A black box or EDR (event data recorder) captures every detail about what happens before airplane crashes.EDR's are automotive black boxes.They can capture speed, braking time, turn signal status and even seat belts.

The Parking Garage Knows More Than You Think

Every single car entering a parking garage has its licence plate photographed.The only problem is that if two cars arrive and leave at the same time they may not be able to exit if the tickets are swapped.

Blog Post 3

The Interview

by: Angie B.

Me: How old were you when you got your first computer?

Dad: 31.

Me: What was your favorite piece of technology growing up?

Dad: A casette recorder.

Me: How has technology changed over your lifetime?

Dad: It's become smaller, more powerful and more affordable.

Me: Have the changes you've seen in technology over your lifetime mostly helped or hurt society?

Dad: Both.

Me: What is your greatest hope for technology in the future?

Dad: I hope society uses it for good.

Me: What is your greatest fear for technology in the future?

Dad: Society will use it for negative things.

Me: What advice would you like to share with the younger generation about technology?

Dad: "Not" can you do it but "should" you do it.

Me Me: Would you buy a robot?

Dad : No.
